Dominick Bruno

I have been using, building, fixing (and breaking) computers and software since the late 1980s. I am currently updating my skillsets as a part-time student at Lambda School's Full Stack Web Development course. While I develop a more recent web portfolio, enjoy the following old designs & check out some of my other work!

Print

Web Dev is definitely my bread and butter, but print is also a passion of mine. Editing, proofing, layout and even some graphics design for the cover - not only is the work fun, it's also rewarding! There's nothing like the feeling of holding a book you made yourself in your hands, and you can perform a valuable service to others as well by spreading knowledge. Click learn more to see a sample of my work.

Community

When communities are strong, everyone benefits. Health improves, productivity increases, and cooperation reigns. This principle of collaboration and sharing my labor with both my family and my community is a cornerstone of my work ethic. "It takes a village to raise a child" applies to development projects too! Get in touch

Print Work

Editing is a wonderful way to really bond with a book and absorb what it has to teach you. I've been privieged to have been a part of two highly successful projects.

Programming from the Ground Up

Programming from the Ground Up is an introductory book which uses Linux and Assembler to teach basic programming concepts.

Content Editor

Used in Princeton University's 2005 CS 217 Introduction to Programming Systems course.

Éire Nua: A New Beginning

Éire Nua: A New Beginning serves as a primer on the Irish-authored Éire Nua proposal for a peaceful, unified Ireland.

Editing, layout, updating figures, info-graphic design; footnoting

Produced for Cumann na Saoirse Náisiúnta as part of a US Congressional lobbying campaign.

Social Work

I'm a firm believer in the right of all people to equal and accessible opportunities to achieve success and happiness in life, and that participating in building a better, more humane world is essential to a well-rounded life.

North American Kurdish Alliance (NAKA)

After helping to promote several successful crowdfunders to rebuild areas of the primarily Kurdish region of Syria known as Rojava, I was honored to be offered a position as Co-Chair of Membership & Parternerships.

One People's Project

I was the original webhost and webmaster for the acclaimed One People's Project - a resource for those fighting hate and bigotry, as well as an early consultant and analyst.

Homeless Empowerment Project / Community Action Team (HEP/CAT)

While homeless, co-founded this non-profit with Jah Burns of Rutgers University to address the issues of povery and homelessness in NJ. Former student volunteers have since spread the operating model nationwide.

Essays & Articles

One of the most important and necessary conditions for the continued growth of society and humanity in general is the wide dissemination of information in an accessible format. The written word is a gift that should be shared freely.

Cracking Voter Fraud

Part narrative, part tutorial detailing how a team of Rutgers interns and myself uncovered voter fraud in New Brunswick, NJ.

Appeared in 2600, vol. 19, #4.

The Registered Voter Leak: Big Deal or NBD?

Continuing a theme; in 2016 a major aggregation of voter registration data was accidentally leaked on the internet. This essay explains what really happened, what concerns are legit or based in ignorance, etc.

Appeared on The Cryptosphere, Canada's premier hacktivism news site.

Press Release: East Tennesseans to Help Bring Electricity to Kurds Fighting ISIS

Press release announcing Tennesseans joining the Rojava Electricity Project crowdfunder. This crowdfunder successfully raised US$23k in 23 days to fund the acquisition of generators for the city of Amuda, Rojava.
